# Brand Scope — Testlify

_Derived from the Testlify brand brain (`projects/testlify/brand-brain/` + `stats.md` / `opinions.md`) for
Step G0 of the competitor study. This is the single shared reference every G2 reasoning agent reads. It is a
**direction-setter, not an allow-list** — if an agent reads a page and sees a strong on-brand subject not
listed here, it should use it._

## 1. What we are / who we serve
Testlify is an **AI-powered skills-assessment and interview platform** for hiring. Companies build a test
from a library of 3,500+ pre-built tests (4,500+ roles) or AI-generate custom ones, run conversational AI
interviews (chat/voice/video), apply anti-cheating proctoring, get AI-scored reports, and push results into
100+ ATS. **Buyer:** hiring managers, recruiters, talent-acquisition teams — startups to enterprise, 50+
industries, screening at volume. **Job they hire us for:** screen candidates faster and more objectively —
remove bias, cut time-to-hire, make every hiring decision **defensible** — without a clunky candidate
experience. Positioning: *"High-confidence talent decisions."*

## 2. Subjects we can credibly own (illustrative — not exhaustive, not a checklist)
- **Skills tests & assessments** — per-role, per-skill (technical/coding, cognitive/aptitude, language,
  personality, role-specific). Our deepest, most ownable territory; free public tests are the niche's #1 link magnet.
- **Pre-employment / skills-based hiring** — what to test, how to screen, validity & fairness, candidate experience.
- **Interview process** — structured interviews, AI interviews, interview questions by role/skill, scorecards.
- **Hiring assessment terminology** — glossary of assessment, psychometric, and hiring terms.
- **Recruitment metrics & analytics** — time-to-hire, quality-of-hire, cost-per-hire, funnel/benchmark data.
- **Bias, fairness & defensibility in hiring** — adverse impact, validity, compliance (EEOC-style), audit trails.
- **Candidate / role templates** — job descriptions, scorecards, assessment rubrics, interview kits.
- **Proctoring & assessment integrity** — anti-cheating, remote test integrity, AI-cheating in interviews.

## 3. Off-limits / not us (directional signal — strong hint to SKIP or TRANSPLANT, not a hard ban)
- **HR-ops not tied to hiring** — payroll, benefits, compensation bands, retention bonuses, PTO policy,
  performance reviews, L&D/upskilling-after-hire (AIHR/TalentLyft's territory).
- **ATS / CRM / sourcing product content** — applicant-tracking mechanics, recruiting-agency CRM, outreach
  sequencing (Recruiterflow/TalentLyft's product lane; transplant the *format*, not the topic).
- **General workplace / management / culture** — leadership, team-building, employee engagement, office life.
- **Pure career-advice-for-jobseekers** — resume-writing tips, "how to get a job" (candidate-side, off-buyer).

## 4. Transplant note
When a competitor earns links on an off-brand subject with a **link-pulling format** (e.g. a compensation
calculator, a retention-bonus glossary, an HR-trends report), **keep the format, point it at our world**
(skills tests, screening, interview process, assessment data). Record the original off-brand subject in
`Notes` (e.g. `transplanted from: compensation`); never let it become our subject. The TRANSPLANT tier exists
precisely so the niche's highest-link-pull formats aren't lost just because their topic sits off-brand.
