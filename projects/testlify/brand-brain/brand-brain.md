## 1. What the company is
Testlify is an **AI-powered skills-assessment and interview platform** for hiring. Companies use it to
screen and evaluate candidates before interviewing them: build a test from a library of **3,500+ pre-built
tests** (covering 4,500+ roles) or AI-generate custom ones, run **conversational AI interviews** (chat,
voice, video), apply **anti-cheating proctoring** (webcam, screen-record, browser lockdown), get
**AI-scored reports**, and push results into **100+ ATS** platforms (Greenhouse, Lever, Recruitee).
Enterprise-grade and compliant: **SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR**.

- **Money pages:** `/pricing/`, `/compare-plans/`, the `/test-library/...` product pages, `/talent-review/`,
  the competitor `/...-alternatives/` and `/testlify-vs-.../` comparison pages, `/integrations/...`.
- **Proof points (from the homepage — confirm/curate in `stats.md`):** 1,500+ teams worldwide · 4.7 on G2 ·
  94% candidate satisfaction · 55% decrease in time-to-hire · 6× recruiter efficiency · 3,500+ tests ·
  4,500+ roles · >80% of applicants complete the test.

## 2. Who we talk to
**Hiring managers, recruiters, and talent-acquisition teams** — from startups to enterprises, across 50+
industries. They're time-pressed and screening at volume (volume hiring, remote hiring, lateral hiring,
campus recruitment). They want to hire faster *and* more fairly, and to defend the decision afterwards. The
writing also reaches **candidates** on test/interview pages, but the buyer it persuades is the recruiter.

## 3. Positioning / value prop
**"High-Confidence Talent Decisions."** Sub-promise (their words): *"The AI-native, enterprise-grade
platform for fair, defensible hiring at scale."* The core promise: screen candidates **faster and more
objectively** — remove bias, cut time-to-hire, and make every hiring decision **defensible** — without a
clunky experience for the candidate.

## 4. Voice & tone
**Dials:** serious · professional but reader-facing (talks to "you") · respectful · matter-of-fact & confident.
**In one line:** a hiring expert talking to a busy recruiter — plain, direct, and confident, not salesy.

| Trait | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Address the reader | Write to "you" / "your" | First person ("I", "we") |
| Formality | Plain, professional words; swap provide→give, require→need | Formal/abstract verbs |
| Vocabulary | Concrete, hiring-domain words | AI slop — seamless, leverage, robust, dynamic, empower, unlock, elevate |
| Sentence voice | Prefer active | Drift into passive (it already runs ~31%) |
| Confidence | State findings as fact | Reflex hedging (the brand rarely hedges) |
| Punctuation | Commas, full stops, single quotes, colon-led lists | Em dashes; exclamation marks; semicolons; emojis |
| Openings | Lead with the reader's problem or a plain definition | "In today's world…"; personal anecdotes |
| Warmth | A light touch — "look", "well", "simply" | Stiff corporate tone; over-chatty |
| Evidence | Back claims with real numbers (see `stats.md`) | Vague puffery |

**Tone by page type:** money/product pages = tight, benefit-led, one clear CTA; blogs/glossary = more
conversational, plain definitions, real examples; competitor/comparison = factual and fair, never trash-talk;
candidate-facing test pages = reassuring and simple.

**Exemplars:** openings lead with the reader's problem; CTAs are "Try for Free", "Book a Demo".
(Full evidence + counts in `voice-analysis.md`.)

## 5. Style rules (concrete)
- **Open** on the reader's problem or a plain definition — not "In today's world…" and not a personal anecdote.
- **Structure:** scannable. H2/H3 sections, colon-led lead-ins, lists, a "how it works" sequence, an FAQ block
  where it fits. Comparison pages: a fair "What is X? / What is Y?" structure.
- **Address the reader** as "you/your". Stay in active voice. State things with confidence.
- **Evidence:** back claims with real numbers from `stats.md` — never round, never invent, flag the ⚠️ ones.
- **Stories/opinions:** at most **one story** (`stories.md`) and **one opinion** (`opinions.md`) per piece,
  each backed by a real number. Use only approved ones.
- **Honesty tell:** say plainly when Testlify is **not** the right fit (e.g. tiny teams, niche needs) — the
  comparison pages already do this, and it builds trust.
- **Vocabulary:** plain words (give not provide, need not require). **No AI slop** — seamless, leverage,
  robust, dynamic, empower, unlock, elevate, optimize.
- **Mechanics:** no em dashes; no exclamation marks; no emojis; single quotes; semicolons rare.
- **CTAs:** "Try for Free" (primary) or "Book a Demo"; demo link → https://hs.testlify.com/meetings/testlify/demo

## 6. Anti-AI guard
- **Banned buzzwords / AI slop:** seamless(ly), leverage/leveraging, robust, dynamic, strategic, innovative,
  empower, unlock, elevate, optimize, transform, delve/dive into. (Testlify's own pages over-use these —
  strip them.) **Banned hollow intensifiers:** truly, honestly, genuinely, really, "the real".
- **No** exclamation marks, emojis, em dashes, tricolons, or over-signposting ("It's important to note…").

## 8. Voice-test rubric (run on every draft)
1. Sounds like a real hiring expert, not a model.
2. Opens strong (reader's problem / plain definition), no "In today's world…".
3. Second-person, benefit-framed, active voice.
4. Has at least one real, sourced number (from `stats.md`).
5. Right structure for the page type (scannable, lists, FAQ where it fits).
6. CTAs correct ("Try for Free" / "Book a Demo"; correct demo link).
7. Clean of AI tells (no slop words, no em dashes, no exclamation marks) — anti-AI pass run.
8. Clean grammar and rhythm; no boilerplate or puffery.
