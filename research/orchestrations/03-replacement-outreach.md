## Goal
Find pages that already hold backlinks but are dead or stale, then become the better destination
those links point to.

## When to run it
You want links where the hard part — earning the link in the first place — is already done. You just
need a superior replacement and a low-friction ask.

## The sequence
1. **Find the target** — pick the variant that fits:
   - Dead pages on expired domains → [[strategies/content-resurrection]] — _SpamZilla to find expired niche domains, then Semrush filters (Active + Follow + Content) to surface live links pointing at the dead pages._
   - Outdated pages on live trusted sites → [[strategies/content-replacement]] — _Google search operators (before:, copyright, inurl:year, etc.) to surface stale pages that still have backlinks._
   - Broken links on resource pages / 404 pages that still have backlinks → [[strategies/resource-page-link-building]] — _the broken-link two-email sequence, plus Semrush → Indexed Pages → Broken Pages to find dead URLs (with backlinks) worth recreating._
2. **Build the replacement** → [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]] — _identify or create a genuinely better version of the dead/outdated content before reaching out._
3. **Pitch on free help / free content** → [[strategies/outreach-value-proposition]] — _tell them their link is broken/outdated and offer your replacement; lead with the "free help" or "free content" value prop._ Operate it with [[strategies/outreach-execution]] — _find/verify contacts, send the help-first sequence, follow up._

## Where the links point
The swapped link points at your replacement asset. Where relevant, let that asset pass value onward
via the reverse-silo (see [[strategies/reverse-silo]]).

## Done when
Linking sites have swapped their dead/outdated link for your replacement URL.
