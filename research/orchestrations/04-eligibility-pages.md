## Goal
Create a specific page that a known group of websites habitually links to, then make yourself
eligible for those links.

## When to run it
You want low-friction links from a predictable linker class. Great supplemental tactic — especially
for local businesses — but finite in volume.

## The sequence
1. **Become eligible** — pick the variant(s) that fit. Each is a "known class of linkers" you qualify for:
   - Donor/sponsor listings → [[strategies/donation-link-building]] — _donate to vetted nonprofits whose donors page links out; qualify the page links out (and is indexed) BEFORE donating._
   - Jobs/careers page → [[strategies/jobs-page-link-building]] — _publish a real or curated jobs page that job aggregators, .edu career centers, and associations link to._
   - Local Chamber of Commerce → [[strategies/local-chamber-of-commerce]] — _join the chamber; verify the member directory links out + is indexed; use consistent NAP-W._
   - Niche/professional associations → [[strategies/niche-business-associations]] — _join (or otherwise earn a listing on) industry associations whose member pages link out — topically + locally relevant._
2. **Find the linker class** → [[strategies/link-prospecting-prioritization]] — _discover and rank the specific sites that give out this category of link (search strings + Semrush bulk/authority scoring)._
3. **Low-friction outreach** → [[strategies/outreach-value-proposition]] — _make the simple ask (add our listing / list us as a donor/member); the value is already built into qualifying._ Operate it with [[strategies/outreach-execution]] — _find the right contact, send, follow up._

## Where the links point
These links typically point at the eligible page itself (donor listing, jobs page). Keep that page
internally linked into the rest of the site so the authority isn't stranded (see [[strategies/reverse-silo]]).

## Done when
Qualified sites have added your listing and the link is live and correct.
