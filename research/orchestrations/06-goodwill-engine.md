## Goal
Earn links by *giving* something the other party wants to publish — a glowing testimonial, or an
award/badge they're proud to display. Both flip the usual dynamic: instead of asking for a link, you
hand over value the recipient *wants* to show off, and the link comes attached.

## When to run it
You're building your own brand (not a hands-off client) and want links that are freely given rather
than extracted. Works at two scales: 1-to-1 (testimonials) and 1-to-many (an awards program). This is
a brand-led campaign — its strength scales with your own credibility.

## The sequence
1. **Praise vendors you actually use** → [[strategies/testimonial-link-building]] — _list products/services you use, confirm their testimonials page links out, and offer a real testimonial (write it only after they accept; nofollow is fine here)._
2. **Hand out recognition at scale** → [[strategies/awards-and-badges]] — _create a credible award/"best of" program (Clutch model); winners embed your badge — often on their homepage — linking back to your winners page._
3. **Frame the give, then operate** → [[strategies/outreach-value-proposition]] — _lead with what *they* get (social proof, recognition, exposure)_ — run through [[strategies/outreach-execution]] for contacts, sending, and follow-up. Keep badge/testimonial anchors branded/generic with [[strategies/anchor-text-optimization]].

## Where the links point
Testimonial links point at your homepage/brand; award-badge links point at the **winners/methodology
page** you control. Internally link that winners page into the rest of the site so its accruing
authority isn't stranded (see [[strategies/reverse-silo]]).

## Done when
Testimonials are published with links, and badges are live on recipient sites (ideally homepages) —
both compounding as you scale the program.
