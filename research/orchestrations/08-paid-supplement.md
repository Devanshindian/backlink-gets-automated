## Goal
Accelerate a campaign by **paying** for links — either inserted into existing pages (niche edits) or
bought through a marketplace — while keeping the risk as low as paid links allow.

## When to run it
Only as a **supplement** on top of a real content-led program — never the foundation. You accept the
risk and have the budget. Content (the [[orchestrations/01-asset-engine]]) must already be doing the
heavy lifting before you layer this on.

> **Warning (carried from the strategies):** paying for links violates Google's guidelines and can be
> penalized. Risk is only *minimized*, never removed. Keep every placement genuinely relevant, keep
> anchors natural, and prefer pointing paid links at content/assets rather than money pages.

## The sequence
1. **Vet targets hard** → [[strategies/link-prospecting-prioritization]] — _score candidates on relevance + real authority/traffic; a paid link from a junk site is worse than no link._
2. **Insert into existing pages** → [[strategies/niche-edits]] — _Ahrefs Link Intersect to find pages that link to top results but not to you; pitch a "paid collaboration" insert into the relevant page._
3. **Hand-pick marketplace links** → [[strategies/buy-backlinks]] — _e.g. Loganix "Shop the List"; vet each domain's backlink + keyword profile in Semrush before buying; point links at content/assets where possible._
4. **Stay under the radar** → [[strategies/anchor-text-optimization]] — _the critical guardrail: vary anchors toward branded/generic, never exact-match-heavy on paid links._ Operate outreach/ordering via [[strategies/outreach-execution]].

## Where the links point
Prefer pointing paid/inserted links at **content or linkable assets** (lower risk), and let the
reverse-silo carry value to money pages (see [[strategies/reverse-silo]]). Hitting transactional pages
directly is higher risk.

## Done when
A small, well-vetted set of relevant paid/inserted links is live with natural anchors — supplementing
(not replacing) the links earned by the other engines.
