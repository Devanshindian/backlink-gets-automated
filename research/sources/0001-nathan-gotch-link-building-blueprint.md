## Summary
Nathan Gotch's end-to-end link-building blueprint. Core thesis: backlinks still matter (foundation
of PageRank; backed by Backlinko/Semrush/Ahrefs studies), but focus on **quality over quantity**.
The system: build linkable assets → acquire links to them → internally link those assets to
commercial pages (the "reverse silo") to spread authority and reduce penalty risk. Covers how to
validate link-bait ideas, asset frameworks that work, asset promotion, prospect prioritization, and
the core outreach principle (WIIFM / win-win).

## Raw notes
- Backlinks still matter: PageRank foundation; Backlinko (DR correlates with rankings), Semrush (more backlinks → higher SERP position), Ahrefs (more backlinks → more organic traffic).
- **Reverse silo:** don't blast commercial pages (risky footprint); acquire most links to linkable assets; assets internally link to commercial pages. Reduces link-penalty risk (most common penalty type).
- **Take inventory first** before creating assets:
  1. Figurehead / personal brand / subject-matter expert (roundups, press, podcasts) — most powerful; Gary Vee ~99% links via personal brand.
  2. Existing free resources (tools/software).
  3. Proprietary data usable for marketing.
- **Validate link-bait ideas:**
  - #1 Total linking root domains — find linkable ideas in your industry (and adjacent industries, then adapt). Method: Semrush broad search → find industry-specific top rankers (ignore broad ones like Home Depot) → Backlink Analytics → Index Pages → sort by domains → find their most-linked pages. HVAC example surfaced a **glossary** (whole glossary + each term page attracted links) and a **calculator**; a government site also had a high-link calculator.
  - #2 User signals — engagement signals an idea could work (imperfect: some social hits don't earn links). Mine Reddit subreddits (Home Improvement → HVAC / AC repair / furnace repair).
  - Use linkability + user signals together; linkability is primary.
- **Proven asset frameworks:** calculators / free tools / software; animated infographics (e.g. "how a furnace works" — hard to make, high payoff); rankings/lists with filters (e.g. ranking law schools); data-driven content (own data best; can also curate, e.g. Exploding Topics).
- Common trait of top-linked pages: **high effort/investment**.
- **Promotion of new asset:**
  1. Internal linking — push existing link equity to the new asset (search site for the topic, add internal links).
  2. Outsource outreach — agencies (recommends Digital PR) as "fuel on the fire" while building in-house capability.
  3. In-house outreach.
- **Prospecting & prioritization:**
  - Referral-traffic principle: "Will this site drive relevant referral traffic likely to convert?" — used to prioritize, not eliminate.
  - Relevancy pyramid (built by Gotch): tiers from 100% relevant (e.g. competing HVAC co. in same city — tiny pool, unlikely) → adjacent local home-services (plumbers, electricians, foundation repair) → home-services in other cities/counties → locally-relevant-but-not-niche (Chesterfield softball league, Ballwin theater). Start at top, work down as tiers tap out.
  - Dream 100: list the 100 best target sites; build relationships slowly; very hard to even find 100 truly high-quality sites in most niches.
  - LLM deep research (ChatGPT) + a good SOP to auto-find opportunities (donation link-building example; ~9 min vs hours).
- **Outreach core principle — WIIFM (what's in it for me, from their side):** every pitch a win-win. Technical factors (email warm-up, send velocity, positioning, copywriting) matter but the value proposition is the lead domino. Incentives: money (#1), free/extended trials, free content written for their site.
- Promotional close: Gotch SEO Academy (117-page SOP, link-bait idea database, outreach template, 2×/week coaching; 391 videos mention backlinks).
