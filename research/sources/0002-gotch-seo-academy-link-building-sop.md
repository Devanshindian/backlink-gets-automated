## Raw notes

### Link Building Principles
- **Referral Traffic Principle:** Does this site send relevant traffic likely to convert? Prioritizer, not filter.
- **Make your website better** — natural links are the best technique.
- **Understand Link Intent** — ~20% of keyword targets should have high link intent; ranking for them triggers passive link acquisition.
- **Content is the only scalable link-building strategy** — techniques get early traction; content earns links passively long-term.
- **More Eyeballs = More Backlinks** — promote every asset through every channel; don't just rely on outreach.
- **Backlinks are fuel on the fire** — keyword selection, technical/UX, on-page all do most of the work; backlinks close the gap.
- **If It's Easy, It's Probably Not Valuable** — citations, directories, web 2.0s = low-value.
- **Limit linking to unlinkable pages** — most commercial pages (category, product, lead-gen) won't earn natural links; concentrate external links on informational assets and use reverse silo.
- **WIIFM** — every outreach email must offer a clear win for the recipient.
- **Do What's Proven to Work (But Be Creative)** — 80% proven frameworks, 20% experimental; "steal" the framework, add a unique spin.
- **Don't Ignore Existing Backlinks** — build authority of pages linking to you before acquiring new ones.
- **80/20** — 20% of content → 80% of backlinks; 20% of outreach → 80% of results; 20% of backlinks → 80% of SEO results.
- **Nofollow links** — pursue if the site sends relevant referral traffic (YouTube example) or is a trusted entity.

### Example Link Building Workflow (flowchart)
Take inventory of existing assets → Find 100 link-bait ideas → Flag toxic backlinks
→ Min: Create (x3) data-driven assets → Min: Create (x1) free tool → Min: Create (x1) visual asset
→ Find Your Dream 100 link opportunities → Send 100 outreach emails → Min: (x5) follow-ups per prospect
→ Create more link bait

### Identify Linkable Assets
Inventory categories: figurehead/personal brand/SME (roundups, HARO, podcasts); alumni; free resources/tools/software; careers/internships pages; events; scholarship pages; data; helpful content (articles, podcasts, YouTube).

### Flag Toxic Backlinks — seven types to scan for manually
1. DoFollow Blog Comments (spammy keyword-stuffed comments with links)
2. Directory Links with Keyword-Rich Anchor Text
3. Spammy Donation Links (e.g. donor lists on low-quality sites)
4. Article Directory Links (thin spun-article directories with keyword-stuffed links)
5. Web 2.0 Links (links buried in Wix/Weebly subdomains or deep pages)
6. Public Blog Networks (high linked-domain count, no organic traffic)
7. Fake .edu/.gov Backlinks (comment-spammed .edu/.gov subpages)

**SEMRush Backlink Audit workflow:**
Semrush → Link Building → Backlink Audit → check "Toxic Domains" → add to Toxic Backlinks tab in dashboard.

**About disavowing:** Not recommended in 99.9% of cases — only consider during a negative SEO attack, and even then it's risky. Goal is awareness, not necessarily action.

### Create Link Bait — Validate Ideas
**Metrics hierarchy:**
| Metric | Validation Score |
|---|---|
| Total Linking Root Domains (avg. RDs the idea type attracts) | High |
| User Signals (social/community engagement) | Medium |
| Search Volume | Low |

**80/20:** 80% of ideas should have proven linkability across any niche; 20% can be experimental.

### Generate 100 Link Bait Ideas — five methods
1. **Study Your Competitors:** Semrush → Backlink Analytics → Indexed Pages sorted by referring domains. Repeat for your own domain.
2. **Like-to-Link Method:** Semrush → Backlink Analytics → Outbound Domains — see what types of content trusted entities link *to*. Find the pattern and replicate it.
3. **Model Other Niches:** Use proven link-bait templates from other industries (database in the course).
4. **Study Trends:** Reddit, Google Trends, Exploding Topics, YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest. Adapt trending topics to your niche.
5. **Use ChatGPT:** Prompt templates — Statistics ("# Statistics on [TOPIC] for [YEAR]"), Syntax-based (interactive|data-driven|statistics|…), Life-Force 8 (Cashvertising biological desires), Secondary Wants. Localized but applicable to any niche.

### Create Answer Bait
Seeds (What / Why / How / Is / Who / When / Where / Can) — generate keyword ideas across question formats; each answer becomes an asset with link-bait potential.

### Specific Asset Types
- **Facts:** "[# Amazing Facts About X]" — high link potential; use ChatGPT to generate 10 ideas.
- **Terms & Dictionary:** Glossary pages — potentially tapped in some niches but still high link potential; example: 888poker Poker Glossary.
- **Rankings:** Tier/ranking pages (e.g. CS:GO rank system) — attract links from players discussing rankings.
- **Quizzes:** Interactive quizzes; use ChatGPT for 10 quiz ideas per niche.
- **Calculators & Estimators:** Compound interest calculator, cost estimators — functional tools with high link pull.
- **Generators:** Business name generator, etc.
- **Marketer Bait:** Content targeting tech/marketing/SEO/advertising blogs. Not 100% niche-relevant but can land powerful links from trusted entities. Controversial or data-driven angles work (e.g. HouseFresh "How Google is killing independent sites" → 30,000 backlinks from 758 domains).
- **Jobs Page:** Create jobs/internships pages to attract links from job aggregators. Or curate job opportunities relevant to your industry.

### Content Resurrection Technique
1. Go to SpamZilla → filter by Majestic Topical Trust Flow for your niche.
2. Run prospective expired domain through Semrush to check quality of its backlink profile (relevant sources, no artificial signals, links hit content, anchor text under-optimized).
3. If quality: Semrush → Indexed Pages → open Backlinks for a top page → filter: Active, Follow, Placement: Content.
4. Export backlinks → add to link-building dashboard (Source = dead page, Opportunity URL = site to reach out to).
5. Identify or create a matching asset.
6. Outreach with broken-link template.

**Outreach template (broken link):**
Subject: Broken link
Hey [NAME], My name is [NAME] and I'm the [POSITION] at [COMPANY/BLOG].
I found your article and noticed that you're linking to a dead resource. We actually just published something similar: [TITLE] and were wondering if you'd like to replace that dead link with our new resource?
I'm confident it will add value for your readers. Please let me know your terms and we're open to negotiation.

### Content Replacement Technique
Use search string templates (e.g. `site:moz.com + "2015"`) to find outdated content on trusted sites.
Create updated asset → pitch it as the replacement.

2. Export results with Data Miner (Chrome extension).
3. Paste URLs into Semrush Bulk Analysis.

### Marketer Bait
Target marketing, advertising, SEO blogs. Angle: "Big [niche] SEO result" case-study guest post. Even without controversy, a local SEO case-study pitch ("we grew a plumbing business's organic leads by X%") can land guest posts and links from SEO/marketing sites.

### Get Interviewed
**Finding opportunities:**
1. Search strings: `"INFLUENCER NAME" + "interview"`, `NICHE + "interview with"`, `inurl:[keyword] inurl:podcast`
2. Semrush → Competitor → Backlinks → filter URL for "interview" or "interview-with"
3. Ahrefs → Competitor → Backlinks → same filter; add DR:30+ filter
4. Spotify / iTunes native search
5. YouTube (same search strings as Google)
6. Matchmaker.fm — podcast-guest database

**Outreach template:**
Subject: Podcast Guest Pitch: [TOPIC // EXPERTISE]
Hi [podcast host name], I'm [name], [job title] at [org]. I [relevant expertise]. I really enjoyed the episode where you discussed [specific episode topic]. [Unique insight]. I believe there is a gap in the conversation [unique value you can add]. I could make an episode on any of these: [HEADLINE #1], [HEADLINE #2], [HEADLINE #3]. I [1-2 sentences credentials]. Please let me know if you're accepting new guests!

**Warm outreach:** Build a relationship first (comment on their content, engage on social) then reach out informally; see warm-outreach email example in source.

### Donation Link Building (complete)
**Qualification checklist (in order):** 1. Do they link out? 2. Is the link followed? (Inspect element;
nofollow OK if valued as a lead channel.) 3. How much to land a link? (Sponsorship tiers, e.g. "Silver –
$3,000 → Logo and link on Sponsor page"; many don't publish cost.) 4. Is the donations page indexed?
**Find:** Strategy #1 Google search strings (`"become a sponsor"`, `inurl:/sponsors $100 AND "501(c)(3)"`,
`allintitle:"sponsors" + CITY`, `site:.edu "student discounts"`, etc. + local org types). Strategy #2
reverse-engineer a known donor in Ahrefs → Backlinks → search "donors"/"sponsors" → filter Dofollow.
**Prioritize:** relevant > authority > (ignore traffic) > cost approved by SEO director > aligns with
client's beliefs (get client approval before any donation).

### Local Chamber of Commerce
Search `[CITY/COUNTY/STATE] chamber of commerce`; confirm member listing links out + page is indexed.
Browse the national Chamber of Commerce Directory for more. Use consistent NAP-W (Name, Address, Phone,
Website) matching the Google Business Profile.

### Niche Business Associations
Search `[CITY] professional associations`, `[CITY/STATE] [NICHE] association`, etc. Confirm outbound
links: Ahrefs → Site Explorer → Outgoing Links → Linked Domains. Find the linking page (member dir /
"100% Club" / sponsors), confirm indexed. Develop strategy: join, or use broken-link / guest-post tactics.

### Testimonial Link Building
Write/record testimonials for products/services you use that link back. Self-brand only (hard for
clients). Process: list vendors → do they show testimonials (dedicated page / homepage)? → do they link
out? → nofollow OK (it's a brand play). Template provided. Only write after they accept + a link is likely.

### Sneaky Web Design Credit Technique
For web designers: add a footer credit / dedicated "Site Credits" page on client sites linking back to
you (WebFX "Web Design Site Credits" example on a law-firm site).

### Link Roundups (content-dependent)
Search `[KEYWORD] + weekly link roundup / link roundup / best blog posts`; reverse-engineer competitors
in Ahrefs → Backlinks → search "roundup". Prioritize by authority + organic traffic. Rule #1: don't
promote garbage. Outreach template (Subject: Your weekly roundup).

### Reverse Expert Roundups
Find existing expert roundups and pitch to be added. Long search-string list (`[keyword] + "expert
roundup" / "expert tips" / "experts share" / "gurus"` …). Ahrefs → search "expert roundup". Template +
Dan Wesley/CreditLoan demo (offered to promote the post to a 130k-follower audience in return). Once
accepted, SME/ghostwriter creates the contribution.

### List Post Link Building
Goal: land on "best/top X" list posts. Search syntax `{top|best} {niche|city} {blogs|tools|courses|
examples|podcasts|experts|…}`. Ahrefs → Backlinks → search "best"/"examples"/"top". Outreach (Subject:
Awesome list!). Tip: use Check My Links to find broken links on the list page as leverage.

### Resource Page Link Building
Goal: land on resource pages. Search strings (`keyword + inurl:resources`, `"best resources"`,
`intitle:links`, `intext:"further reading"`, etc.). Ahrefs → search "resources". Reach out: broken-link
two-email sequence (Part 1 offer to flag dead link → Part 2 reveal it + propose your replacement) OR
no-broken-link template (Subject: Your awesome resource page). Variant: Semrush → Indexed Pages → Broken
Pages → find 404s with backlinks → recreate asset → outreach to linkers.

### The Merger Technique 2.0
WARNING: don't use expired domains anymore (risk too high). Acquire a live site/tool and 301-redirect it
into yours. Find: `best free [NICHE] {calculators|tools|software}`, `best [NICHE] blogs` (inactive);
build a list of ≥100 targets (WebFX, Neil Patel/ubersuggest examples). Vet: editorial link profile
(Ahrefs Dofollow sorted by DR), under-optimized anchor text, Archive.org history, indexed
(`site:domain.com`). Execute: Phase 1 outreach ("Interested in selling your website?"); Phase 2
negotiation (DR 40–69 ≈ $200, DR 70 ≈ $300; pay pennies on the dollar; counter template); Phase 3 build
a superior asset if no deal; Phase 4 merge (Semrush export Indexed Pages → map Old→New URL → page-by-page
301; WordPress Redirection plugin imports a .csv; create supporting pages e.g. gotchseo.com/facebook-stats/).

### Write Guest Posts
Get links in the body (not just author bio). Search `list of guest posting sites`, `[KEYWORD] "Guest
post"`, `"Write for us"`. Ahrefs → search "/author/", "guest post", "contributor". Important: never write
upfront (get approval); pitch unique uncovered ideas. Template (Subject: Guest post ideas! + 3 ideas).
Create: SEO content brief (or Rankability) → write/outsource → inject link via "3 awesome resources"
framework (your link is #2).

### Respond to Journalist Requests
Featured (HARO successor) emails daily opportunities. Not great client-side (low success, needs SME);
great for own brand. Math: 1–2/day ≈ 365/yr × ~25% = ~91 links. Also test Muck Rack, Qwoted,
helpab2bwriter.com; Twitter/LinkedIn #journorequest / #prrequest + search strings (`"looking for an
expert" quote`, etc.).

### Niche Edits
Link inserts into existing pages. Ahrefs → Keyword Explorer → Link Intersect: paste top linked-to pages
for your keyword, set "but doesn't link to" = your page → domains not linking to you. Outreach template
(Subject: Paid Collaboration with [brand]) explicitly acknowledges an editorial/administrative fee → paid,
grey-hat.

### Oprah Technique (Interview Experts)
Host your OWN podcast and interview experts (The SEO Life example; podcast homepage + episodes earn
backlinks; guests link to their episodes). Build list (experts must have own websites: `best [NICHE]
podcasts/experts/blogs`), prioritize by authority (start micro-influencers), get prepared (Blue Yeti mic,
Zoom/Riverside, iMovie, Anchor distribution, Google how-to). 3 outreach templates (social DM + 2 emails).

### Create Awards & Badges
Clutch.co model: give awards/badges that recipients embed (often on homepages) and link back. ChatGPT to
brainstrom categories ("link bait campaign like clutch.co… 10 ideas for a plumber in St Louis" → "St.
Louis Plumbing Innovation Awards"). Badges often year-stamped → annual renewals.

### Advanced Link Building Techniques (passive, at scale)
- **Micro Website:** tiny free tool on its own domain (Netflix fast.com ≈ 22.6K RDs; links back to homepage).
- **Extreme Documentation:** exhaustive help center (facebook.com/help 343,000+ RDs; /business/help 75,000+).
- **UGC Strategy:** companies link to their social profiles sitewide → compounding (facebook.com/wix
  561,000+, twitter.com/wordpress 73,000+, a university LinkedIn 7,700+).
- **Privacy Policy Technique:** ad platforms require advertisers to link policy/cookies → millions of links
  (facebook.com/privacy 100,000+, stripe.com/privacy 56,000+, pinterest 35,000+, myadcenter.google 7,500+);
  also "Payment Providers" and "Analytics".

### Vetting & Prioritizing Link Prospects (point system)
13-variable point system (5 best → 1 worst), three groups: Relevance (topic/link/keyword), Metrics (site/
content/outbound quality, editorial guidelines, traffic growth, total organic traffic, total RDs, DR), and
Resources (ease to acquire, potential cost). Higher score → higher priority. Lives in the dashboard's
"Reference - Point System" tab.

### Relationship Building & Outreach
**Find contact info:** Hunter.io (domain pattern + verify), Voila Norbert (name+domain), Fiverr; also social
media and joining their newsletter (catches personal emails). **Validate:** Hunter (.csv) or Fiverr. **Send:**
1) Track everything (log dates in "Outreach #" columns). 2) Get a branded email (`john@nike.com` >
`joe@linkbuildinggurus.com`); if not, make an outreach domain without SEO/marketing lingo.

### Link Building FAQs + Troubleshooting
**Poor outreach results:** email deliverability (warm up an aged company email; test spam score via
hunter.io/email-verifier, mail-tester.com, mailgenius.com, glockapps.com); subject lines (lead with value —
"Quick question NAME", "14 broken links NAME :("); weak value prop; poor page selection (only promote awesome
pages, not category/product/lead-gen); lack of personalization. **Time to results:** crawl ≈1–2 weeks, then
≈4–8 weeks to see movement.

### Anchor Text Optimization
Anchor types with effectiveness (NE/SE/E/VE/EE) + risk: Branded (NE/EE depending on domain; low/high risk),
Generic (NE, low), Naked Link (NE/EE; low/medium), Brand+Keyword (E, low), Variations (E, medium), Partial
Match (VE, medium), Exact Match (EE, HIGH). More effective = riskier. **Anchor Text Cycling:** rotate types
across links. Site #1 (bad 👎: all exact-match/naked) vs Site #2 (good 👍: blended branded/partial/LSI/
generic/naked). Exact/partial-match *domains* make branded/naked anchors effectively exact-match → higher risk.
