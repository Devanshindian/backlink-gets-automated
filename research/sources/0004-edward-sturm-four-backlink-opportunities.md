## Summary
Edward Sturm's daily marketing podcast (ep. 958) walks through **four backlink opportunities**:
(1) **image link building** — a Charles Floate technique: create original images, upload them to DR-90+
free stock-photo sites with a link in the attribution/credit field, and earn natural backlinks +
reverse-image-search reclamation; (2) **Favikon** — a paid influencer-marketing platform whose profile
gives a *do-follow* link (DR 44); (3) **Featured.com** — the journalist-request platform, whose profile
gives a *no-follow* link (DR 41) but is useful for shaping topical authority; and (4) **Grokipedia** —
xAI's AI-generated Wikipedia challenger (DR 57) whose references are *do-follow* and currently easy to
earn. All four are genuine backlink tactics (in scope). Includes a flagged black-hat image variant and
a flagged paid-indexing mention.

## Raw notes
- **Image link building (Charles Floate):**
  - Step 1 — Create original images: phone photos, Canva/Photoshop — product shots, niche/location
    photos, data visualizations, infographics, charts.
  - Step 2 — Upload to free stock sites (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Flickr, Creative Commons; DR 90+);
    put your website link in the **attribution/credit** field. Bloggers/journalists/designers pull them
    daily → free natural backlinks, zero outreach, compounding.
  - Step 3 — Periodically **reverse image search** (Google / TinEye) to find uncredited uses → simple
    email asking for a source-credit link (or automate it). Very high conversion; natural anchor text.
  - **Black-hat variant (flagged, not recommended):** find popular images with no clear ownership /
    watermark, claim them, upload under your profile, send attribution requests to everyone using them.
- **Favikon:** connects creators with brands for influencer marketing; brands value micro-influencers.
  DR 44. Make a profile → **do-follow** link to your site + socials. ~$29 for one month (can keep the
  profile after). Optional: keep paying and earn brand collaborations (which can also yield links).
- **Featured.com:** connects journalists seeking quotes with experts. Profile = **no-follow** link, DR 41.
  Even though no-follow, use the profile's brand description to shape **topical authority** for your
  target keywords. Host's answer acceptance rate ≈ 79%. (Core tactic lives in
  [[strategies/haro-journalist-requests]].)
- **Grokipedia:** xAI's AI-generated Wikipedia challenger. DR 57. References are **do-follow** (Wikipedia
  is nofollow). Easy to get an entry right now, even with a small following. **Nuance:** value depends on
  the entry being **indexed** — link to it, share it, get clicks. Paid indexing (e.g. Rapid URL Indexer)
  exists but is **black-hat / risky — not recommended.**
