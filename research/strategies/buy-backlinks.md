## 1. What it is
Paying for backlinks — e.g. through a marketplace like **Loganix's "Shop the List"**, where you
hand-pick the linking sites.

> **WARNING (from the source):** Buying backlinks is **against Google's guidelines**. There's always
> risk. You can only *minimize* it by doing it as naturally as possible. This is a black-hat-adjacent,
> flagged tactic — documented for awareness and supplemental use, not as a foundation.

In plain terms: you can pay to place links, but Google forbids it and can penalize you. If used at all,
it should *supplement* the legitimate techniques in this knowledge base — content stays the foundation.

## 2. Why it works (and the catch)
- A relevant link from an authoritative page passes ranking value regardless of how it was obtained.
- **The catch:** paid links violate guidelines; detection or a manual review can trigger penalties. The
  whole game is buying *quality, relevant* links that look natural — which is hard and risky.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- Only as a supplement to a content-led, white-hat program — never the core strategy.
- You accept the penalty risk and have vetted the marketplace/sites carefully.
- Related grey-hat tactic: [[strategies/niche-edits]] (paid link inserts).

## 4. End-to-end process
1. **Select a keyword/page to rank.** Safest: drive bought links to **content / linkable assets**
   (informational pages). Higher risk: pointing them at transactional/money pages.
2. **Look for opportunities.** In a marketplace like **Loganix → "Shop the List"**, filter for your
   niche (e.g. search "SEO" / "Marketing" tagged sites) and hand-pick candidates. The list shows
   DF/NF (dofollow/nofollow), DR, DA, TF, CF, RD, traffic, niche, ETA, and price per site.
   _tool(s): loganix_
3. **Vet each prospective domain in Semrush.** Examine the **backlink profile** (Referring Domains —
   are they real, relevant, editorial?) and the **keyword profile** (Organic Research → Positions — does
   it actually rank for relevant keywords and get traffic?). Only buy where both profiles look healthy.
   _tool(s): semrush_
4. **Keep it natural.** Vary anchors toward branded/generic ([[strategies/anchor-text-optimization]]),
   keep placements relevant and in-content, and don't over-concentrate on money pages.

## 5. Tools
- [[tools/tools#loganix]] — "Shop the List" marketplace to hand-pick paid placements.
- [[tools/tools#semrush]] — vet each domain's backlink + keyword profiles before buying.

## 6. Success metrics
- Quality (relevance, real traffic, editorial profile) of purchased links — not raw count.
- Ranking movement on the target page (allow 4–8 weeks), watched against penalty-risk signals.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Guideline violation / penalties** — the headline risk; only minimized, never removed.
- **Buying junk** — many marketplace sites have fake traffic or spam profiles; the Semrush vetting step
  is essential.
- **Over-optimization** — exact-match anchors on bought links is a strong manipulation signal.
- **Pointing paid links at transactional pages** — riskier than pointing them at content assets.
