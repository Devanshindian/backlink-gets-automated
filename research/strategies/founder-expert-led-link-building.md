## 1. What it is
Using a real person from the business — the founder, a recognizable face, or a genuine expert — to
earn links by showing up in places that link back. Three main places: **expert roundups** (articles
that quote several experts on a topic), **press inquiries** (journalists asking experts for quotes),
and **podcasts** (which usually link to the guest in the show notes).

In plain terms: people and publications link to *people* they trust. A known expert is a magnet for
mentions in a way a faceless company rarely is.

## 2. Why it works
- A recognized expert naturally attracts editorial mentions and links. Gotch points to Gary
  Vaynerchuk, who earned ~99% of his backlinks just from the strength of his personal brand.
- He calls this "by far the most powerful technique you can use."

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- There is a willing founder/expert with genuine authority who can participate publicly.
- Identified during the asset-inventory step of [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]].

## 4. End-to-end process
1. Identify the figurehead/expert and the topics they can credibly speak on.
2. Get them into expert roundups (find roundup opportunities in the niche).
3. Respond to press/journalist inquiries — pitch quotable expert commentary.
4. Book podcast appearances (which typically include a backlink in show notes).
5. Recycle the expert's contributions into owned linkable assets.

## 6. Success metrics
- Links/mentions earned per appearance; authority of placing domains.
- Volume of roundup/podcast/press placements over time.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- Dependent on the expert's availability and willingness — a bottleneck.
- Requires real expertise; thin "expert" positioning won't get accepted.
