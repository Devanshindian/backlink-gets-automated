## 1. What it is
Journalists and bloggers regularly ask for expert sources/quotes. Services like **Featured** (a
successor to the old HARO — "Help A Reporter Out") email you these requests; you respond with a quality
answer, and if the writer uses it, you get credited with a link.

In plain terms: reporters need experts to quote. You answer their questions; when they publish, they
link to you as the source. It's a digital-PR tactic — links come from real editorial coverage.

## 2. Why it works
- Links come from genuine media/editorial coverage, often on high-authority domains — strong signals.
- You're providing real value (expert input a journalist needs), so it's white-hat by design.
- It builds brand authority beyond the link.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- **Best for building your *own* brand.** It's a poor fit for client-side link building: low success
  rate, and you need a dedicated subject-matter expert (SME) who can write quality responses.
- You can commit time daily — it's a numbers game.
- The downside: you'll spend time on responses that never get picked up.

## 4. End-to-end process

### The math (why consistency matters)
- 1–2 submissions per day ≈ **365 submissions/year**
- At a ~**25% conversion rate** ≈ **91 new backlinks** from quality sources

Block out time to write quality responses **daily**.

### Sources of requests
- **Featured** — sends potential link/brand opportunities daily.
- Also test **Muck Rack**, **Qwoted**, and **helpab2bwriter.com** (Help a B2B Writer).
- **Social search** — on Twitter/X and LinkedIn, follow `#journorequest` and `#prrequest`, or use
  native search strings like:
  - `"looking to talk to" interview`
  - `"is there anyone" interview`
  - `"is there anyone quote"`
  - `"looking for an expert" quote`
  (Example seen in the SOP: a reporter tweeting "Looking to feature 10 SEO agency owners on an article
  about the challenges of scaling an SEO agency. #SEO #prrequest #journorequest".)
_tool(s): featured, qwoted, muck-rack, help-a-b2b-writer_

### Respond
Answer quickly, concisely, and with genuine expertise. Quality + speed wins placements. The SME (or a
ghostwriter briefed by the SME) should craft each response.

### The fully-automated path (BacklinkerAI)
Source 0003 demonstrates **[[tools/tools#backlinker-ai]]**, a tool that runs this whole loop on
autopilot on top of Featured. You set up a **persona** once (name, LinkedIn, company, job title,
website, bio); the AI then auto-writes and submits expert pitches as that persona to incoming
journalist requests, and a dashboard tracks them (pitches → selected/in-review → published). In the
demo it produced ~17 published backlinks over two months on domains of DR 70–92, at an advertised
~$8/link after the discount code.

**Trade-offs vs. doing it by hand:** you trade control and per-answer quality for volume and zero
effort, and you're trusting an AI to represent your (or a client's) expert persona accurately. The
video is a vendor promo with an affiliate code and self-reported numbers — the *tactic* is legitimate,
but vet the actual placements (relevance, authority, that the quote isn't generic/fabricated) before
leaning on it, especially for clients.

## 6. Success metrics
- Submissions per day/week (input metric — keep it consistent).
- Pickup/conversion rate (~25% is the SOP benchmark) and authority of resulting links.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Inconsistency** — it only works as a daily habit; sporadic effort yields little.
- **Low-effort responses** — generic answers don't get picked; lead with a unique, quotable insight.
- **Client-side mismatch** — set expectations: low success rate, SME-dependent, brand-first.

## 9. Automation hooks
- Aggregate requests from Featured/Qwoted/Muck Rack + social `#journorequest`/`#prrequest` searches
  into one queue, filtered by niche relevance (high).
- LLM-assisted first-draft responses for the SME to refine (partial).
- Tracking submissions → pickups in a CRM (high).
- **End-to-end automation** via [[tools/tools#backlinker-ai]] — persona-based auto-pitching on Featured,
  with a built-in pitch/published dashboard (full, with the quality/authenticity caveats above).
