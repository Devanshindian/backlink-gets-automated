## 1. What it is
Create original images, upload them to free stock-photo sites with a link back to your site in the
**attribution/credit** field, and earn backlinks two ways: (a) passively, every time someone uses your
image and credits you, and (b) actively, by reverse-image-searching your photos to find people using
them *without* credit and asking them to add a source link.

In plain terms: you scatter your images (each carrying your link) across the busiest photo libraries on
the web, then collect the credit links they generate — like leaving business cards in places people are
already shopping for pictures.

## 2. Why it works
- Free stock-photo sites (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Flickr, Creative Commons) are **domain rating 90+**
  (DR = a 0–100 estimate of a domain's link authority), and bloggers, journalists, and designers pull
  images from them every day — so your image (and its credit link) spreads with **zero outreach**.
- Each use that credits you is a **natural, editorial backlink** with natural anchor text (the visible
  text of a link) — exactly the kind search engines trust.
- It **compounds**: the more images you seed, the more uses and link opportunities appear automatically.
- The reclamation half has an **unusually high conversion rate** — the person already used your image,
  so they know they owe you the credit; you're just asking them to make it right.
- Floate's angle: in an era flooded with AI images, *original* photos/graphics stand out more, and most
  of the industry has forgotten this old tactic.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- You can produce original visuals: phone photos, or graphics in Canva/Photoshop.
- Works in almost any niche — product shots, location/niche photos, **data visualizations,
  infographics, charts**.
- Great low-cost, passive supplement to active campaigns; pairs with [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]]
  (infographics/data viz are linkable assets) and is a form of link reclamation like recovering
  unlinked mentions.

## 4. End-to-end process

### Step 1 — Create original images
Take original photos on your phone, or make graphics in Canva/Photoshop: product shots, niche-specific
and location photos, data visualizations, infographics, charts — anything relevant to your topic.

### Step 2 — Seed them on free stock sites (with your link in the credit field)
Upload to **Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Flickr, Creative Commons**, etc. In each platform's
**attribution / credit** field, link back to your website. These DR-90+ platforms then distribute your
images to people who credit you on use. _tool(s): free-stock-image-sites_

### Step 3 — Reclaim uncredited uses (reverse image search)
Periodically **reverse image search** your photos on Google Images or **TinEye**. You'll find sites
using them without proper attribution. Send a simple email: *"Hey, love that you used my image — would
you mind adding a source-credit link?"* Conversion is high because they know credit is owed. This step
can be fully automated (find uses → templated outreach). _tool(s): tineye_

## 5. Tools
- [[tools/tools#free-stock-image-sites]] — DR-90+ libraries (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Flickr, Creative
  Commons) to host images with an attribution link.

## 6. Success metrics
- Number of images seeded; number of natural credit links earned (passive).
- Uncredited uses found per reverse-image-search pass and credit-link conversion rate (active).
- DR of sites picking up your images.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Generic/AI-looking images** get ignored — originality is the whole edge.
- **No link in the credit field** = no benefit; double-check each platform's attribution settings.
- **Black-hat variant (documented for awareness only — do NOT use):** finding popular images with no
  clear ownership/watermark, claiming them, uploading them under your profile, and sending attribution
  requests for images that aren't yours. This is deceptive and risky; the white-hat version is powerful
  enough on its own.
