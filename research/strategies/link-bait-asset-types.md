## 1. What it is
A catalog of content *formats* that have a strong track record of earning backlinks across many
different industries. These aren't generic blog-post ideas — they are specific *types* of pages
that other websites habitually link to when writing about a topic.

In plain terms: think of these as templates. The exact topic changes per niche, but the *shape*
of the asset is proven to attract links because people naturally want to reference useful tools,
comprehensive glossaries, authoritative ranking pages, and surprising facts.

This strategy complements [[strategies/link-bait-generation-methods]] (how to generate ideas)
and [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]] (how to validate and build them).

> 📊 **Swipe file:** ~75 real, proven link-bait examples (with URLs, type tags, and fill-in **headline
> templates** like "How [TOPIC] Works" or "# [TOPIC] Statistics and Trends [Updated YEAR]") are in
> [[sources/0005-seo-marketing-database]] → **"Templates - Link Bait"** tab — use it to pick a format
> and a headline pattern for each catalog entry below.

## 2. Why it works
- Each format earns links for a structural reason — it's a *reference* (glossary, facts, rankings)
  or a *utility* (calculator, generator, quiz). Reference and utility pages attract citations from
  people writing about the topic.
- High-effort, interactive, or data-rich assets signal investment — which is the common trait of
  top-linked pages per Gotch's analysis. Thin assets rarely attract links.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- Choose from this list when picking what to build next from your link-bait idea backlog.
- Pick formats that are validated for your niche (see [[strategies/link-bait-generation-methods]]).
- Most formats require real investment in content depth, design, or interactivity — budget accordingly.

## 4. Asset format catalog

### 1. Answer Bait (informational articles answering specific questions)
Question-keyword pages ("Can I pour bleach into the sink?", "Why is my furnace making noise?").
Each page answers one clear question comprehensively.
- **Why it earns links:** writers cite them as references when answering similar questions elsewhere.
- **ChatGPT generation:** use the What / Why / How / Is / Who / When / Where / Can seed lists from [[strategies/link-bait-generation-methods]].
- **Volume strategy:** build series (20+ question pages on one sub-topic) to dominate a niche cluster.

### 2. Facts Pages
Format: "[Number] [Adjective] Facts About [Topic]" — e.g. "12 Shark Facts That May Surprise You".
- **Why it earns links:** writers and bloggers cite facts when making a point; readers share them.
- **Variants:** `[TOPIC] facts`, `facts about [TOPIC]`, `{Amazing|Fun|Interesting|Cool} facts {about|for} [TOPIC]`.
- **ChatGPT prompt:** "Give me 10 ideas for 'Facts'-related content for a [NICHE] company in [CITY, STATE]."
- **Example:** NOAA Fisheries "12 Shark Facts That May Surprise You".

### 3. Terms & Dictionary / Glossary
A comprehensive glossary of industry terms, each with its own page or section.
- **Why it earns links:** the most-referenced glossary in a niche becomes *the* go-to reference; every new article on any term can link to it.
- **Bonus:** each individual term page is itself a link-bait asset, multiplying potential entry points.
- **Note:** potentially competitive in some industries — validate first.
- **Example:** 888poker's Poker Glossary (A–W, each entry a separate page).

### 4. Rankings / Tier Lists
A page that ranks or tiers items with clear criteria (rankings, tiers, leaderboards, award lists).
- **Why it earns links:** people love referencing rankings; subjects of the ranking often share/link.
- **Variants:** "Best X in [City]", "[Industry] Rankings [Year]", tier/badge lists (e.g. CS:GO Ranks).
- **Interactive filtering** dramatically increases link pull — let users sort by region, size, year, etc.
- See also [[strategies/link-bait-asset-types#6-awards-badges]] — rankings and awards overlap.

### 5. Quizzes
Interactive quizzes where readers test their knowledge or get a personalized result.
- **Why it earns links:** novelty + shareability; sites link to quizzes as engagement resources.
- **Examples:** "Do You Know Your Home's Plumbing?", "Weight Loss Body Type Quiz" (Dr. Berg).
- **ChatGPT prompt:** "Give me 10 quiz ideas for a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] in [CITY]."
- **Implementation tools:** Typeform, Interact, custom-built quiz widgets.

### 6. Calculators & Estimators
Free tools where the user inputs numbers and gets a useful result.
- **Why it earns links:** calculators are cited as utilities; writers link to them as "see how much it costs" or "use this calculator".
- **Examples:** Compound Interest Calculator, HVAC cost estimator, plumbing repair cost estimator.
- **High effort, high payoff** — the technical investment deters competitors and concentrates links.

### 7. Generators
Tools that generate something for the user (business names, taglines, color palettes, etc.).
- **Why it earns links:** useful, reusable, shareable — same logic as calculators.
- **Examples:** Business Name Generator, slogan generators, local event idea generators.
- **Implementation:** often a simple form + JS logic or a ChatGPT API wrapper.

### 8. Marketer Bait
Content targeting marketing, advertising, SEO, and tech blogs — even if your primary niche is
unrelated (e.g. plumbing).
- **Why it earns links:** marketing/SEO bloggers are *very* generous linkers. A compelling case study
  or data piece can land links from highly trusted domains that wouldn't normally cover your niche.
- **Two angles:**
  - **Controversy / David vs. Goliath:** HouseFresh published "How Google is killing independent
    sites like ours" → 30,000 backlinks from 758 domains. High risk, high reward.
  - **Case study / local SEO result:** pitch to SEO/marketing blogs with a guest post on how you
    grew a local business's organic traffic by X% — these get accepted readily.
- **Outreach angle:** "Are you accepting guest posts? We just conducted an SEO campaign for a
  plumbing business and grew its organic leads by X% and would love to share how we did it."

### 9. Data-Driven / Original Research
Publishing proprietary data, surveys, or industry studies.
- **Why it earns links:** journalists and bloggers cite statistics — they need a source URL.
- **Own data is best** (e.g. survey your customers). Curated/compiled data from public sources also works.
- **See also:** [[strategies/link-bait-generation-methods]] Method 5 (statistics prompt template).

### 10. Animated / Interactive Infographics
Visual explainers of complex processes, animated or highly designed.
- **Why it earns links:** publishers embed or screenshot them; impossible to replicate without credit.
- **High production cost = high link barrier** — competitors rarely copy them.
- **Example:** "How a Furnace Works" animated diagram.

### 11. Jobs / Internships Pages
See [[strategies/jobs-page-link-building]] for the full process.
- **Why it earns links:** job aggregators and local resource pages link to active job listings.

## 6. Success metrics
- Referring domains earned per asset within 90 days of launch.
- Whether sub-pages (individual glossary terms, individual calculator results) also earn links.
- Compare against estimated RDs from the validation step.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Low effort = no links.** A thin glossary with 5 terms or a calculator that doesn't actually work
  will earn nothing. Every format here requires genuine investment.
- **"Overplayed" ideas** — if a competitor already owns the link-market share for a format in your
  niche, differentiate rather than compete head-to-head.
- **Marketer bait controversy** — the controversy angle (attacking a large platform) can backfire PR-wise; weigh reputational risk vs. link reward for the business context.
