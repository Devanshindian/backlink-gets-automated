## 1. What it is
A repeatable research process for generating a large backlog of **validated** link-bait ideas —
content assets engineered to attract backlinks — before you spend budget building any of them.

The target is **100 ideas**; you won't build them all, but having a big validated backlog means you
always have proven options to pick from.

In plain terms: this is the "what should we build?" step. [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]]
is the "how do we build and validate it?" step. [[strategies/link-bait-asset-types]] is the
catalog of formats that reliably earn links.

## 2. Why it works
- You generate ideas *before* committing resources, so you only build what has evidence of working.
- Borrowing proven *formats* from competitors (not their exact topics) gives you a shortcut: if a
  glossary attracted 400 referring domains for an HVAC competitor, a glossary probably works in
  that niche.
- ChatGPT and trends tools act as unlimited ideation partners once you have the right prompts.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- Run this *after* [[strategies/toxic-backlink-audit]] and *before* building any new asset.
- Needs at least one competitor with a visible backlink profile (Semrush/Ahrefs access).

## 4. End-to-end process

Target: fill the "Link Bait Ideas" tab in the dashboard with 100 entries (Idea | Source | Estimated RDs | Estimated Cost).

### Method 1 — Study Your Competitors (strongest signal)

**Navigation:** Semrush → enter competitor domain in the top search bar → set scope to **Root Domain** → click **Search** → click the **Backlink Analytics** section (left sidebar, under Competitive Research) → select the **Indexed Pages** tab.

The **Indexed Pages** tab has these columns:
`Title and URL | Backlinks ↕ | Domains ↕ | External Links | Internal Links | Last Seen`

There is also a **"Broken Pages"** checkbox to filter for dead pages (useful for Content Resurrection).

1. Click the **Domains** column header to sort descending (most referring domains first).
2. Scan the top 10–20 rows. Note the *format type* of each page — not the topic, but the kind of page:
   - Is it a glossary? A calculator? A statistics round-up? A ranking list? A free tool?
3. For each format type you spot, add an entry to the "Link Bait Ideas" tab:
   `Idea description | Source (competitor URL) | Estimated RDs (from the Domains column) | Estimated Cost`
4. Repeat for 3–5 competitors.
5. **Run your own domain too.** Formats that have already attracted your biggest link counts should get more assets built around them.

_tool(s): semrush, ahrefs_

### Method 2 — Like-to-Link Method (what do trusted sites link *to*?)
Instead of looking at what competitors *receive* links to, look at what they *link out to*.

**Navigation:** Semrush → enter a trusted niche entity's domain → Backlink Analytics → select the **Outbound Domains** tab (it sits in the same tab row as Overview | Network Graph | Backlinks | Anchors | Referring Domains | Indexed Pages | **Outbound Domains**).

The Outbound Domains view shows:
- Each outbound destination domain the site links to
- And for each one, you can expand to see: `Source page Title and URL | Ext. Links | Int. Links | Anchor and Target URL`

This reveals *which types of content* that trusted site chooses to cite — their editorial linking behaviour. For example, if backlinko.com consistently links to statistics pages and original-data studies on Hubspot, that's a strong signal that statistics/data pages earn links in the marketing niche.

1. Identify the 3–5 most common content formats in the outbound links.
2. Add those formats to your ideas tab.
3. Repeat with 3–5 different trusted sites; recurring patterns = highest-confidence ideas.

_tool(s): semrush_

### Method 3 — Model What's Worked in Other Niches
Many link-bait formats work across industries. Take a proven template from a different niche and
adapt it to yours.

**ChatGPT prompt:**
> Give me 10 link bait ideas using this format: [TEMPLATE] for a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] in [LOCATION]

Example:
> Give me 10 link bait ideas based on this proven framework: "57 NEW [TOPIC] Statistics" for a
> plumbing company in Houston. Clarification: It doesn't need to use the exact format.

_tool(s): chatgpt_

### Method 4 — Study Trends
Trending topics have built-in audience attention — adapt them to your niche.

**Sources to monitor:** Reddit (subreddits for your niche + broad ones like r/news), Google Trends,
Exploding Topics, YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest.

**Process:**
1. Extract a seed idea from a trending topic (e.g. a Reddit post about a surprising substance poured
   down a sink → sparks an entire content series about what can/can't go down drains).
2. Ask: "What version of this trend is relevant to my niche?"
3. Use ChatGPT to scale one seed into 10–20 variations.

**Reddit example — seed extraction in action:**
Search "into the sink" on Semrush with Exact Match filter and you get dozens of real queries people
are asking:
- "can i pour cacl2 into the sink"
- "can i pour nickel chloride into the sink"
- "can you pour bleach into the sink"
- "can you dump the hello fresh pack into the sink"

A single Reddit thread asking about one substance can reveal an entire content series. Each query
above is a standalone blog post answering a specific question — the series as a whole earns links
because it becomes the definitive resource on the topic.

**Bonus tip:** Start niche-relevant, but also watch broadly trending topics (e.g. economic recession
content) — sometimes you can adapt broad trends into niche angles:
- "How to Extend the Life of Your Plumbing Systems During Economic Hardship"
- "Water Conservation Tips to Reduce Your Bills During a Recession"

_tool(s): reddit, chatgpt_

### Method 5 — Use ChatGPT Prompt Templates
A library of prompts for generating large volumes of ideas quickly:

**Statistics / data-driven:**
> Give me 10 link bait ideas for a [NICHE] company in [CITY], [STATE] that uses this exact
> template: "# Statistics on [TOPIC] for [YEAR]"

**Syntax-based (pick one format):**
> Give me 20 link bait ideas for "{interactive | data-driven | statistics | survey-driven |
> investigative study-driven | long-form | ego bait | social currency | award-driven | case-study |
> newsjacking | visual | controversial | practical tools | game | practical calculators | practical
> templates | practical cheat sheets | emotional assets that tap into positivity | emotional assets
> that tap into anger | emotional assets that tap into excitement | emotional assets that tap into
> sadness | story-driven}" assets for an [NICHE] business located in [CITY] [STATE].

**Life-Force 8** (Cashvertising's eight biological desires — pick what's relevant):
> Give me 20 link bait ideas that tap into this human desire: "{survival | enjoyment of life | life
> extension | enjoyment of food and beverages | freedom from fear, pain, and danger | sexual
> companionship | comfortable living conditions | to be superior, winning, or keeping up with the
> Joneses | care and protection of loved ones | social approval}" for an [NICHE] business located
> in [CITY] [STATE].

**Secondary Wants** (learned desires):
> Give me 20 link bait ideas that tap into this human desire: "{to be informed | curiosity |
> cleanliness of body and surroundings | efficiency | convenience | dependability / quality |
> expression of beauty and style | economy / profit | bargains}" for an [NICHE] business located
> in [CITY] [STATE].

_tool(s): chatgpt_

### Create Answer Bait (bonus method)
Use question-keyword seeds to generate ideas that double as link bait *and* informational SEO pages.
Seed word groups:

| Seed | Examples |
|---|---|
| What | what is, what are X used for, what happens when, what's the difference between |
| Why | why is, why are, why do, why can't |
| How | how much, how long, how to, how often, how far |
| Is | is X worth it, is X safe, is X good/bad for you |
| Who | who is the best, who invented, who made |
| When | when should, when does, when did |
| Where | where is, where can, where to |
| Can | can I, can you, can X [go bad / cause / be reversed / make you sick] |

For each seed + niche topic, you can generate entire content series. A local plumber could do "Can
I pour [substance] into the sink?" as a series of 20+ posts, each a separate link-bait answer piece.

## 6. Success metrics
- Size of the validated idea backlog (target: 100 ideas before starting builds).
- Estimated RD potential per idea (proxy: avg. referring domains of top 3 existing examples of that format).
- % of built assets that attract ≥ 5 referring domains within 90 days.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Copying the competitor's exact topic** instead of the underlying format — you'll compete head-to-head rather than owning a unique angle.
- **Chasing search volume** as the primary signal — search volume has a *Low* validation score for link bait; a keyword can get lots of searches but inspire no one to link to it.
- **Generating ideas without estimating cost/effort** — add Estimated Cost to every row so you can prioritize by ROI.
- Ignore "overplayed" ideas where one competitor already owns most of the link-market share for that exact format — differentiate.

## 9. Automation hooks
- Method 1 & 2: fully automatable via Semrush/Ahrefs API — pull top pages by referring domains per competitor, cluster by page type (high).

- Full pipeline: competitor scrape → cluster by format → ChatGPT expansion → structured backlog (high).
