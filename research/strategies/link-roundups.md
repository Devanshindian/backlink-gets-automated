## 1. What it is
A **link roundup** is a recurring blog post (often weekly) where a publisher curates and links to the
best new content in a niche. This strategy is about getting *your* content included in those roundups.

In plain terms: some bloggers regularly publish "here are the best posts I found this week" lists. If
you have genuinely great content, you email them and ask to be considered. It's content-dependent —
you need something worth featuring.

## 2. Why it works
- Roundup links are editorial — the curator chose to include you, which search engines value.
- Roundups exist *to link out*, so the publisher is already in a linking mindset (low friction).
- Most roundups are niche-specific, so the links are relevant.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- You have a genuinely strong, recently-published asset worth promoting (see
  [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]]).
- **Rule #1 — don't promote garbage.** The content must add unique value. Promoting weak content burns
  bridges and hurts your credibility with curators you'll want to pitch again.

## 4. End-to-end process

### 1. Find opportunities (search strings)
- `[KEYWORD] + weekly link roundup`
- `[KEYWORD] + link roundup`
- `[KEYWORD] + best blog posts`

### 2. Reverse-engineer competitors
**Navigation:** Ahrefs → **Site Explorer** → competitor domain → **Backlinks** → search `roundup`
(or the other variations above) in the referring-page filter. This surfaces every roundup that has
linked to your competitor.
_tool(s): ahrefs_

Most roundup opportunities will be relevant, so prioritize by **website authority** and **organic
traffic**.

### 3. Reach out
> **Subject:** Your weekly roundup
>
> Hey [blogger name],
>
> I noticed that you do a weekly roundup on your blog (and I absolutely love it).
>
> Are you currently accepting submissions?
>
> We just published a blog post about [INSERT TOPIC], which [UNIQUE VALUE].
>
> Let me know if it's a good fit for your roundup, thanks!
> [Your Name], [Your Position at Company]

## 5. Tools
- [[tools/tools#ahrefs]] — competitor Backlinks filtered by "roundup" to find recurring curators.

## 6. Success metrics
- Roundup inclusions per outreach batch; reply/acceptance rate.
- Authority + traffic of the roundup sites that include you.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Pitching weak content** (Rule #1) — damages relationships and your reputation.
- **One-and-done thinking** — roundups recur, so a good first placement can become an ongoing channel;
  nurture the relationship.
