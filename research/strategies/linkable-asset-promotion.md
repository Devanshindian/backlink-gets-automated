## 1. What it is
The step that turns a freshly published linkable asset into actual backlinks. Even the best asset
earns nothing on its own — you have to actively push it in front of people who might link to it.

In plain terms: publishing a great page and waiting is like opening a shop and never telling anyone.
Promotion is how you get the first visitors (and links) through the door.

## 2. Why it works
- **Internal linking** gives the new page an instant head start by passing it some of the authority
  your existing pages already have (their "link equity" — the trust/ranking value they've built up).
- **Outsourced PR** adds scale fast ("pouring fuel on the fire"), while doing your own **in-house
  outreach** builds a skill you keep forever.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- A linkable asset exists — see [[strategies/linkable-asset-creation]].
- Works hand-in-glove with the [[strategies/reverse-silo]].

## 4. End-to-end process
1. **Internal linking:** search your site for the asset's topic and add internal links from relevant
   existing pages to the new asset (tap existing equity first). _tool(s): site search_
2. **Outsource outreach:** engage a PR agency (Gotch recommends Digital PR) to land premium links at
   scale and help promote the asset. _tool(s): digital-pr_
3. **In-house outreach:** run your own outreach using [[strategies/link-prospecting-prioritization]]
   and [[strategies/outreach-value-proposition]].

## 5. Tools
- [[tools/tools#digital-pr]] — outsourced premium link building / PR.

## 6. Success metrics
- Time-to-first-link after publishing; referring domains gained per asset.
- Internal links pointing to the asset.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- Skipping promotion ("it will fall on deaf ears").
- Over-relying on outsourcing without building in-house capability.
- Vet any paid/agency links for quality — quality over quantity; avoid low-authority "mommy blog" links.
