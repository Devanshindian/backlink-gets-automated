## 1. What it is
Finding industry or professional associations relevant to your niche (e.g. a metropolitan bar
association for a law firm) and earning a link from their member directory or member-recognition pages
(such as a "100% Club" of fully-participating firms).

In plain terms: like [[strategies/local-chamber-of-commerce]], but instead of a general "all local
businesses" chamber, you target organizations specific to your *industry*. These links are both
locally and topically relevant, which makes them especially strong.

## 2. Why it works
- The link comes from a domain that is *topically* relevant to your niche (a legal association linking
  to a law firm) — a stronger relevance signal than a generic directory.
- Association sites are trusted, established entities, so their links carry weight.
- Membership/recognition listings are editorial and expected, so there's no penalty risk.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- Your business belongs to an identifiable profession or industry that has associations.
- You can qualify for membership (some associations require credentials).
- Pairs with [[strategies/local-chamber-of-commerce]]; overlaps with [[strategies/resource-page-link-building]]
  and [[strategies/guest-posting]] for sites where simply joining isn't enough.

## 4. End-to-end process

### 1. Find opportunities
Use these search strings:
- `[CITY] professional associations`
- `[CITY] [NICHE] association`
- `[CITY] [NICHE] business association`
- `[STATE] [NICHE] association`
- `[CITY] association`
- `[NICHE] association`

### 2. Confirm the association links out
**Navigation:** Ahrefs → **Site Explorer** → enter the association's domain → **Outgoing Links** →
**Linked Domains**. This lists the external domains the site links to. If it links out generously
(beyond just social media and its own tools), it's a candidate.
_tool(s): ahrefs_

### 3. Find the specific linking page
Locate the actual page that would carry your link — a member directory, a recognized-members page
(e.g. a "100% Club"), or a sponsors page. Confirm member entries link to member websites.

### 4. Make sure the page is indexed
Do a `site:` check or paste the page URL into Google. If the member page isn't indexed, the link won't
pass value.

### 5. Develop your strategy
Sometimes you can simply **join** the association and get listed. If membership alone won't earn a
link, use the other techniques on these sites instead:
- [[strategies/resource-page-link-building]] / broken-link outreach
- [[strategies/guest-posting]] (pitch a contribution to the association's blog)

## 5. Tools
- [[tools/tools#ahrefs]] — Site Explorer → Outgoing Links → Linked Domains, to confirm the
  association links out before investing.

## 6. Success metrics
- Followed, indexed links from topically + locally relevant association domains.
- Number of relevant associations identified per niche/city.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Assuming membership = a link** — always verify the directory/recognition page links out and is
  indexed first.
- **Ignoring eligibility** — some associations gate membership behind credentials; confirm you qualify.

## 9. Automation hooks
- Discovery: generate `[CITY/STATE] [NICHE] association` queries + SERP scrape (high).
