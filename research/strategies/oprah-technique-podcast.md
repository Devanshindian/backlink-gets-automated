## 1. What it is
Start your *own* podcast in your industry and interview experts/influencers. Named after Oprah because
*you* become the host with the platform. (Example: Gotch's "The SEO Life" podcast, where he interviews
SEO experts.)

In plain terms: rather than pitching to appear on others' shows ([[strategies/get-interviewed]]), you
run the show. Both the podcast itself *and* individual episodes attract backlinks — and guests often
link to (or share) the episode they appear in.

This is the host-side complement to [[strategies/get-interviewed]] (guest side) and a channel within
[[strategies/founder-expert-led-link-building]].

## 2. Why it works
- The podcast homepage accrues links over time (the SOP's example episode page had 33 dofollow links
  from 12 referring domains).
- Each guest is a relevant expert who often links to or promotes their episode — earning you links from
  *their* sites (individual guest episode pages each picked up their own referring domains).
- Hosting builds authority and relationships you can leverage for other tactics.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- You (or your figurehead) can credibly host conversations in the niche.
- You can commit to producing episodes consistently.
- Some basic equipment/tools and ongoing time.

## 4. End-to-end process

### 1. Build a list of experts/influencers
**They must have their own website** (that's where the backlink comes from). Search strings:
- `best [NICHE] podcasts` ← also reveals interview/guest opportunities
- `top [NICHE] experts`
- `best [NICHE] blogs`
- `best [NICHE] experts to follow`
Reverse-engineer via [[tools/tools#ahrefs]] as needed.

### 2. Prioritize by authority (start small)
Don't chase the top names first. Build credibility with **micro-influencers** in the vertical, then use
that track record as leverage to land bigger experts once your show is established.

### 3. Get prepared (bare-minimum kit)
- **Microphone** — e.g. a Blue Yeti.
- **Recording tool** — Zoom works; **Riverside** gives higher-quality recordings.
- **Audio editing** — iMovie (free on Mac).
- **Distribution** — **Anchor** (now Spotify for Podcasters) to publish/distribute.
- Search Google to learn the mechanics of starting a podcast.
_tool(s): riverside, anchor_

### 4. Outreach to guests
**Social media DM:**
> Hi [Name]! I run [the show]. I'd love to have you on to talk about [topic] if you're into it. Is
> there an email where I could send details? All the best, [NAME], [YOUR COMPANY]

**Email (version A):**
> Hi [Name], I host a podcast called [PODCAST NAME], where I interview [NICHE] experts (like yourself).
> Except these aren't those boring interviews where they ramble on about the weather and irrelevant
> topics. Instead, we get into the nitty-gritty of [TOPIC/NICHE] and I'd love to get your insights!
> Please let us know when you'd be available to chat, and thanks for considering!

**Email (version B — warmer):**
> NAME! Hope things are going well with [COMPANY/BLOG]. I'm launching a new podcast "[PODCAST NAME]" and
> you were the first person I thought of to have on as a guest. Let me know if you have any availability
> so I can pick that big brain of yours, thanks!

## 6. Success metrics
- Backlinks to the podcast homepage and to individual episode pages.
- Guest-side links/shares earned per episode; authority of guest domains.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Targeting top names too early** — start with micro-influencers and build leverage.
- **Inconsistent publishing** — the link/relationship compounding only happens with a steady cadence.
- **Guests with no website** — no backlink; prioritize guests who can link back.
