## 1. What it is
Create a profile or entry on an established, high-authority platform that lets you include a link to
your site. Because the platform's domain is already trusted, the link (or the topical-authority signal)
carries weight with little effort. This file tracks specific opportunities; expect it to grow.

Key terms: a **do-follow** link passes ranking value ("link juice"); a **no-follow** link tells search
engines not to pass that value — but it can still help by sending traffic and shaping how a search
engine understands what your brand is about (**topical authority**). **DR/DA** (Domain Rating / Domain
Authority) is a 0–100 estimate of a domain's strength.

## 2. Why it works
- The link sits on a domain Google already trusts, so even a single profile link can matter.
- Several of these are **do-follow** (Favikon, Grokipedia), which directly pass ranking value.
- Even **no-follow** profiles (Featured) let you describe your brand with your target keywords, shaping
  topical authority and earning referral traffic.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- Quick, low-effort wins to diversify your link profile; good for a new or thin backlink profile.
- You can write a clear brand description and (for some) pay a small fee.
- Complements the eligibility-style tactics in [[strategies/local-chamber-of-commerce]] and
  [[strategies/niche-business-associations]] (same "get listed for a link" idea, different platforms).

## 4. End-to-end process (by platform)

### Favikon (favikon.com) — do-follow, paid
A platform that connects creators with brands for influencer marketing (brands value micro-influencers —
small but committed, trusted audiences). DR ~44.
1. Create a profile → you get a **do-follow** link to your site **and** your socials.
2. Requires paying for at least one month (~**$29**) to create the profile; you can keep the profile
   afterward.
3. Optional upside: keep the account to land **brand collaborations**, which can earn money *and*
   additional links (brands sometimes link to creators who praised them). Trade-off: collaborations may
   require recommending other companies. _tool(s): favikon_

### Grokipedia (grokipedia.com) — do-follow wiki entry
xAI's AI-generated challenger to Wikipedia. DR ~57. Unlike Wikipedia (which uses no-follow), Grokipedia's
references are **do-follow**, and entries are currently easy to obtain even with a small following.
1. Get an entry created that references your site.
2. **Crucial nuance:** the link is only valuable if the entry is **indexed** by Google — so link to your
   entry from other places, share it, and get people to click it (clicks help URLs get indexed).
3. Aim for the entry to show up for your brand name. _tool(s): grokipedia_

### Featured (featured.com) profile — no-follow + topical authority
Separate from answering journalist requests (see [[strategies/haro-journalist-requests]]), simply
creating a Featured profile gives a **no-follow** link (DR ~41). Use the profile's brand description to
include the keywords you target so it reinforces your **topical authority**. _tool(s): featured_

## 6. Success metrics
- Do-follow links acquired (Favikon, Grokipedia) and whether the Grokipedia entry is **indexed**.
- Whether the brand entry ranks for your brand name; referral traffic from profiles.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Unindexed Grokipedia entry** = no value; always confirm/encourage indexing.
- **Paid indexing services** (e.g. Rapid URL Indexer) are **black-hat / risky — not recommended**;
  documented for awareness only.
- **Paying for Favikon and forgetting** — you only need one month to get and keep the profile link.
- **Treating no-follow as worthless** — Featured's profile still shapes topical authority and traffic.

## 8. Conflicting views
- Some practitioners argue **no-follow links can still pass some SEO value**; treat as an open debate,
  not a guarantee (raised in source 0004 re: the Featured no-follow link).

## 9. Automation hooks
- Discovery of new profile/platform opportunities + bulk profile creation where APIs allow (partial).
- Grokipedia indexing nudges (sharing/click-seeding) — partly manual.
