## 1. What it is
An **expert roundup** is an article that compiles several experts' tips on a topic. The *reverse*
version flips the usual flow: instead of running your own roundup, you find roundups that are
*already published* and pitch the author to add *your* expert contribution (and a link to you).

In plain terms: someone already did the hard work of building a popular "X experts share their tips"
post. You ask them to squeeze you in. Because the post already ranks and has links, getting added is
high-value with relatively little effort.

## 2. Why it works
- The roundup already exists, ranks, and has authority — you're adding yourself to a proven asset.
- It's editorial and relevant (you're a real expert contributing real value).
- You can offer a clear win in return (e.g. promoting their post to your audience).

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- You have a credible figurehead / subject-matter expert (SME) with a genuine, unique tip — see
  [[strategies/founder-expert-led-link-building]].
- The SME (or a ghostwriter) can create the contribution once accepted.

## 4. End-to-end process

  `[keyword] + "insights"`

### 2. Reverse-engineer competitors
**Navigation:** Ahrefs → **Site Explorer** → competitor → **Backlinks** → search `expert roundup`.
_tool(s): ahrefs_

### 3. Reach out
> **Subject:** Quick question...
>
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> My name is [FIRST & LAST NAME], [POSITION] at [COMPANY], [SHORT ELEVATOR PITCH].
>
> While I was googling for some information about [TOPIC] tips, I came across your expert roundup post
> "[TITLE]" - [NAKED URL]. It is brilliantly written and collated. One of my favorite [TOPIC] experts
> [THEIR NAME] is featured in the article and I have to admit you did an amazing job!
>
> However, as I was reading, I started wondering if you would be open to including my [TOPIC] tips
> among the other participating experts. Being [CREDIBILITY], I believe I can add just that extra bit
> of value to your post.
>
> Let me know if you're interested to hear my thoughts on [UNIQUE TECHNIQUE RELATED TO POST TOPIC] and
> I'd love to share it with you and your readers.
>
> In return, I'd be more than happy [WHAT THEY'LL GET].
>
> What do you think?
> Best regards, [YOUR FIRST & LAST NAME], [YOUR POSITION], [WEBSITE]

The "in return" offer is the key lever. The SOP's worked example (Dan Wesley / CreditLoan.com) offered
to **promote the roundup to a 130,000-follower social audience** in exchange for inclusion.

### 4. Create the contribution
Once the terms are accepted, have the SME (or a ghostwriter) create and submit the contribution.

## 6. Success metrics
- Inclusions secured per outreach batch; authority of the roundup pages.
- Quality of the "what they'll get" exchange offered.

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Generic flattery** — reference a *specific* expert already in the post (as the template does) so it
  reads as genuine, not templated.
- **No value in return** — pair the ask with a concrete benefit (exposure, promotion) or it gets ignored.
