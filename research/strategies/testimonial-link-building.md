## 1. What it is
Writing (or recording) a genuine testimonial for a product or service your company actually uses, in
exchange for being featured on the vendor's testimonials page — which typically links back to your
website.

In plain terms: companies love showing off happy-customer quotes. If you give a vendor a glowing,
authentic testimonial, they'll often publish it with your name, photo, and a link to your site. You
were already a customer, so the praise is real — the link is just the natural credit. (Ahrefs, for
example, runs a testimonials carousel crediting customers like "Gael Breton, Co-founder at
AuthorityHacker," with a link.)

## 2. Why it works
- Testimonials are editorial and trust-building; vendors *want* to publish them, so the link is freely
  given rather than extracted.
- It doubles as brand-building and relationship-building with other companies in your industry.
- It's free and fast — no content to create on your own site.

## 3. When to use / prerequisites
- **Only effective for promoting your *own* brand.** It's hard to get a client to hand over a list of
  their vendors and write testimonials on their behalf, so this is a poor fit for client-side work.
- You actually use products/services worth praising.

## 4. End-to-end process

### Process
1. **List relevant products/services your company uses** — software, tools, suppliers, agencies.
2. **Check how they display testimonials** — a dedicated testimonials page, or quotes somewhere
   (often the homepage)?
3. **Confirm they link out.** Inspect an existing testimonial: does the customer's name/company link
   to their website? If there's no link, skip — you'd get a brand mention but no backlink.
4. **Nofollow is acceptable here.** Because testimonial link building is primarily a
   marketing/brand-building play, a nofollow link (one search engines are asked not to count for
   ranking) is fine — the exposure and relationship still have value.

Only reach out to vendors that clearly link out when you leave a testimonial.

### Outreach (test and personalize)
> Hey [NAME],
>
> My name is [your name] and I'm the [position] at [website].
>
> Just wanted to reach out and let you know how much we love [their product/feature] because it's
> helped us [the benefits you've experienced].
>
> I'd love to write (or record) a testimonial for your website to spread the love. Let me know!
> Thanks, [Your name]

**Important:** only write or record the testimonial once they've **accepted** and you're confident a
link will result. Don't do the work upfront.

## 5. Tools
- None required (browser inspect element to check for the link; email for outreach).

## 6. Success metrics
- Testimonials published with a link back (followed or nofollow).
- Relationships started with relevant vendors (secondary benefit).

## 7. Risks & pitfalls
- **Writing the testimonial before confirmation** — you may spend effort and get no link.
- **Expecting it to work for clients** — it's a self-brand tactic; clients rarely supply vendor lists.
- **Faking praise** — only testify about products you genuinely use; fabricated reviews damage trust.
